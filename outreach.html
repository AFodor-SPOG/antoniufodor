Science Communication

Working on science is a collaboritive effort of many communities. For this reason, outreach and communication is an integral part of my research. By engaging the public, we build understanding for the broader scientific ecosystem which relies on public investment. Outreach is an extension of research and mentorship, particularly for students and early-career scientists navigating scientific pathways. As a student, participating in my high school's Science Olympiad team made me want to pursue physics going forward.

Solar Eclipse Viewing

University of Toledo | 2024

Helped organize and run a campus-wide public viewing event for the 2024 total solar eclipse, which reached 100% totality in our area. Worked with faculty and other graduate students on event coordination, public engagement, and on-site activities while also answering astronomy questions for attendees throughout the eclipse.

LDT Decadal Public Talk Series

University of Toledo | 2023

Presented an online public talk as part of a series celebrating a decade of scientific observations with the Lowell Discovery Telescope. Discussed and answered questions on my research of shocked post-starburst galaxies, highlighting my observing campaign with the telescope.

Cedar Point’s Physics, Science, and Math Days Outreach

Cedar Point | 2023

Helped organize my university’s participation in Cedar Point’s Physics and Math Days, supporting outreach demonstrations designed to make physics accessible and engaging for visitors through hands-on activities and discussion.

Solar Eclipse Viewing

Albion College | 2017

Helped organize a public viewing event for the 2017 solar eclipse in collaboration with the Albion College Astronomy Club. Assisted with distributing eclipse glasses, operating solar-filtered telescopes for safe solar viewing, and showing attendees sunspots visible on the Sun before and after the eclipse.
